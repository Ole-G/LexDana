Comprehensive U.S. IP Data Coverage

LexDana searches across 15+ years of verified U.S. intellectual property records from federal courts, USPTO, and PTAB databases. Every dataset updates within 48 hours of official filings.

Four Core Datasets, One Natural Language Interface

LexDana provides conversational access to the most comprehensive collection of U.S. IP data available. Ask questions across litigation, administrative proceedings, and prosecution records—get answers that connect entities, outcomes, and strategies across the entire IP landscape.

PTAB Proceedings

Complete coverage of Patent Trial and Appeal Board proceedings including IPR, PGR, CBM, and derivation proceedings. Track institution rates, final written decisions, judge panel composition, petitioner strategies, and patent owner responses.

What you can search:

  • Institution and denial rates by judge panel, technology area, or law firm
  • Petitioner and patent owner success rates
  • Prior art combinations and claim construction strategies
  • Attorney and firm track records in PTAB matters

Example queries:

  • "Show institution rates for Judge Driscoll's panel in pharmaceutical patents over the last 2 years"
  • "Has this petitioner's firm ever filed IPRs on continuation patents? What was their success rate?"

Example queries:

  • "What's Judge Gilstrap's claim construction reversal rate in software patent cases?"
  • "Show Quinn Emanuel's track record in the District of Delaware over the last 5 years"

U.S. District Court Litigation

Patent infringement cases filed in federal district courts nationwide, including party information, law firm representation, judge assignments, case outcomes, and procedural milestones.

What you can search:

  • Judge tendencies on claim construction, summary judgment, and Daubert motions
  • Law firm performance by venue, technology area, and case type
  • Settlement timing and patterns for specific parties or venues
  • Case outcomes including jury verdicts, settlements, and dismissals

ITC Investigations

International Trade Commission investigations involving patent infringement, including complainants, respondents, ALJ assignments, preliminary and final determinations, and exclusion orders.

What you can search:

  • ALJ decision patterns by technology area and party type
  • Law firm representation in ITC matters with outcome data
  • Violation findings and exclusion order issuance rates
  • Complainant success rates by industry and patent type

Example queries:

  • "Which ALJs had the highest violation finding rates in consumer electronics cases?"
  • "Show Fish & Richardson's ITC track record in standard essential patent cases"

Example queries:

  • "Top prosecution firms for biotechnology patents in California"
  • "Show examiner allowance rates for semiconductor applications in Art Unit 2800"

Patent Prosecution

Patent application filings, prosecution history, allowance rates, office action patterns, and examiner statistics across all technology centers and art units.

What you can search:

  • Law firm prosecution performance by technology area
  • Examiner allowance rates and typical office action patterns
  • Filing trends by company, technology classification, or time period
  • Average prosecution timelines by art unit or examiner

Data Quality and Updates

Verified, Current, and Traceable

Every data point in LexDana traces to official public records. We don't generate or infer information—we organize and connect existing data from authoritative sources.

Update Frequency

All datasets refresh every 48 hours from source databases. Each result displays the last update timestamp.

Source Verification

Every result includes direct citations to case numbers, docket entries, PTAB decisions, or USPTO records.

Data Accuracy

We continuously monitor data quality and validate entity matching across datasets to ensure accuracy.

Cross-Dataset Intelligence

Connect the Dots Across IP Proceedings

LexDana's power comes from linking data across all four datasets. Track how the same patent moves from prosecution through district court litigation to PTAB review. Follow law firms across different venues and proceeding types. Analyze company IP strategies across multiple jurisdictions.
